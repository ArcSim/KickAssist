## 1.0.0

### New Features
- **Kick Marker Picker**: A quick popup to choose which raid marker is your interrupt assignment.
- **Announce to Group**: Call out your kick marker in party or raid chat with one click, with the marker icon shown right in the message. The message text is editable.
- **Three Ready Macros**: Three draggable macro boxes in the window: Focus + Kick, Set Focus (mark only), and Auto Tab Kick (tab-interrupts a casting mob without losing your current target). Drop any of them straight onto your bars, no digging through the macro list.
- **Auto Macro Sync**: Your macros stay pointed at your chosen marker automatically, and they never remove or overwrite a mark already on the target when you press them again.
- **Per-Spec Interrupt**: The kick macro fills in the correct interrupt for your class and spec (Wind Shear, Counterspell, Mind Freeze, Counter Shot, Muzzle, and the rest) and updates when you change spec.
- **Macro Templates**: Each macro has its own template list in the editor, picked from the "Editing" dropdown. Focus + Kick offers the default (set focus, re-press to kick), a Ctrl-to-kick-your-target version, and a mouseover version; Set Focus offers target or mouseover; Auto Tab Kick has its own. Hold Ctrl to kick your current target instead of your focus.
- **Ready Check Popup**: On a ready check, the picker opens and your kick is automatically called out to the group, so everyone knows their interrupt before the pull. Once a key is underway it stays quiet, so it never causes a chat error.
- **Instance Filter**: Choose which instances the ready-check popup and announce fire in: Mythic+, Mythic, Heroic, Normal, or Raids. The default is Mythic dungeons only.
- **Import Existing**: Pull any of your existing macros' commands in as a starting point. For Focus + Kick and Set Focus, an "Add / Sync {kick} Marker" button adds the marker line for you, or swaps the number in an existing /tm line for your chosen marker, then keeps it in sync.
- **Minimap Button and Options Panel**: Open everything from a minimap button or the in-game AddOns options page.
